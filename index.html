Interesting Facts about Camel

  • It's ears are furry
  • It can move easily across the sand because of it's specially designed feet
  • When they find water, they'll drink as much as possible

Interesting Facts about Chameleon

  • A Chameleon is a lizard like reptile of the genus Chamaeleo
  • It uses it's long tail when moving in the trees to grab a branch and secure its's position
  • Out of the 160 different species of chameleons, over half of them are native to the island of Madagascar

Interesting Facts about Pigeon

  • Studies have found that pigeons, unlike most other birds on the planet, can recognize themselves.
  • Pigeons are Very Fast
  • Pigeons are Spiritual to Some Religions

Interesting Facts about Teddy Bear

  • The first toy bear is believed to be made by German toy maker Margarete Steiff in 1902 that was a jointed, stuffed one
  • It was the 26th President of the US, Theodore “Teddy” Roosevelt, who was the inspiration behind the first teddy bear
  • The first British Teddy Bear Festival was held in 1989 in London

Interesting Facts about Panda

  • Pandas are native to south-central China
  • The typical Panda grows from 1.2 m to 1.9 m
  • An estimated 1,864 pandas still roam the wild, while 400 are kept in captivity

Interesting Facts about Cicada

  • Adult Cicadas only live 4-6 weeks before they die
  • Brood X cicadas are seen every 17 years
  • Female cicadas are silent, we hear the male’s mating call.
